Once a respected and revered figure in the world of Ludum Concordia, Varcen Omirion was the original founder and Grand Arbiter of the system. His vision was simple yet profound: to create a world where differences could be settled through games rather than violence, where justice was based on skill, wit, and fairness. For many years, his dream flourished. Under his rule, Ludum Concordia became a beacon of order, bringing unity to fractured cities and factions. However, as time passed and the system expanded, the Council of Arbiters—once a noble group of impartial judges—became infiltrated by corruption. Wealth, power, and influence began to corrupt even the most principled Arbiters. The ideals Varcen had worked so hard to build were perverted, and his vision turned into a tool of control, oppression, and greed.

As the Arbiters’ greed grew, Varcen began to notice discrepancies in the games themselves. Initially, it was subtle—games manipulated in favour of wealthy merchants, outcomes skewed to benefit noble houses—but soon it escalated. The Arbiters were no longer just overseers; they were the puppet masters, controlling not only the games but the very fates of entire cities.

Varcen, a man who had dedicated his life to fairness, was horrified. He tried to expose the corruption to the public, but his words were dismissed as paranoid rants. The Arbiters, afraid of losing their power, turned on him. They branded him a traitor, accusing him of trying to undermine the very system he had created. In a matter of weeks, Varcen was cast aside, and a public trial was held. The Council used the occasion to solidify their power, portraying Varcen as a delusional figure who had lost his way.

Varcen’s exile was not merely physical; it was symbolic. He was erased from the history of Ludum Concordia, his name struck from every official record, his legacy tarnished. His once-illustrious title was reduced to a shadow, a cautionary tale to those who would question the will of the Council.

Now living in the shadows, Varcen remains a figure of legend—a ghost who walks the fringes of society. He is believed to be dead by most, but in truth, he has been carefully hiding in plain sight. He has lived in exile, moving between cities and settlements, gathering knowledge and allies, waiting for the right moment to strike back.

His time in hiding has not been without cost. The Arbiters’ Sentinels actively search for him, and he must constantly stay one step ahead of them. But in his exile, Varcen has grown wiser, more patient. He knows the Council’s weaknesses—their infighting, their greed, their fear of the truth coming to light—and he’s ready to use those against them.

He’s spent years compiling evidence of the Arbiters’ crimes, but without a trusted force to expose it, the corruption continues. Varcen now seeks the aid of outsiders, people who are not bound by the twisted laws of Ludum Concordia. He sees the players as his final hope—individuals capable of unravelling the web of corruption from within.
