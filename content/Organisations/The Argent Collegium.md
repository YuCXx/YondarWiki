The Argent Collegium is the unchallenged ruling body of the Popolis Despotate, a council of scholars, arcane theorists, and bureaucratic masterminds who wield knowledge as both a shield and a weapon. More than just an academic institution, the Collegium is a machine of governance, ensuring that the pursuit of knowledge remains within the bounds of order, efficiency, and—above all—control.

The Argent Collegium operates on a singular belief: Knowledge is power, and power must be controlled. To the Collegium, unrestricted discovery leads to anarchy, and anarchy is a threat to civilization itself. Their rule is not one of open tyranny but of carefully engineered stability.

- Education is heavily regulated—while all citizens have access to basic learning, higher education is a privilege granted only to those who prove their loyalty and intellect.
- Magical research is restricted, with dangerous or reality-altering spells outlawed. Experimentation beyond sanctioned limits is met with severe punishment.
- The past is curated—historical records are maintained by the Lexoscribes, and some say certain truths have been erased from history entirely.

For all its structure and control, the Argent Collegium is not without cracks. Factions within the institution vie for influence, some advocating for greater freedom in research, while others push for even harsher restrictions. There are whispers of a schism among the Prime Magisters, a secret war fought not with steel, but with ideas, blackmail, and sabotage. Beyond its walls, dissent grows—unsanctioned researchers operate in the shadows, stolen manuscripts circulate in the black markets of Greybark Road, and rumours spread of a lost archive, where the Collegium’s greatest secrets are hidden.

### Structure & Hierarchy
The Collegium is a meritocratic autocracy, where power is earned through intellect, innovation, and political cunning. It is divided into several distinct orders, each overseeing different aspects of governance and research.

#### The Prime Magisters
The highest echelon of the Collegium, an elite council of twelve whose word is law. They control policy, regulate magical advancements, and dictate the direction of Popolis’ scholarly pursuits. Their identities are known, but their deliberations are conducted behind closed doors.

#### The Lexoscribes
Archivists, bureaucrats, and lawmakers responsible for drafting and enforcing the region’s laws. They oversee Exilium and maintain the Edicts of Knowledge, strict regulations on what research is permitted and what is deemed too dangerous.

#### The Luminaries
A sect of Collegium-sanctioned researchers, artificers, and mages who push the boundaries of magic and science—though only within the strict parameters set by the Prime Magisters. Breakthroughs are closely monitored, and unauthorized discoveries often lead to sudden disappearances.

#### The Concordants
The Collegium’s enforcers and inquisitors, who ensure that rogue scholars, heretical theories, and forbidden knowledge are swiftly dealt with. Often disguised as fellow academics, they root out dissent from within, ensuring absolute compliance.
