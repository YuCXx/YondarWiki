# Yondar
Yondar is a land of contrasts and conflicts, innovation and ancient mysteries. Spanning vast territories, it is home to scholar cities, lawless frontiers, and forgotten ruins from civilizations long past. The land itself is known for its unpredictable geography, with landscapes shaped by both natural forces and arcane phenomena. Rivers shift course without warning, mountains seem to grow overnight, and hidden valleys appear only under the right celestial alignments.

## History
### The First Epoch – The Lost Age
Little is known about Yondar’s earliest inhabitants, save for the ruins they left behind—massive stone structures etched with unknown symbols, arcane conduits still humming with dormant energy, and buried archives of forgotten knowledge. Some scholars believe an ancient civilisation once wielded power over reality itself, while others argue that these ruins are the remnants of multiple lost cultures.

### The Rebirth
After the the First Epoch, the continent was plagued by endless feuds—between merchants disputing debts, noble houses vying for influence, and rival factions locked in cycles of revenge. Wars were waged over petty grievances, and blood stained the streets where words should have sufficed. It was during these tumultuous times that the four nations of the [[Popolis Despotate]], the Kingdom of Zakrosia, the Nmoendan Theocracy, and Driecia were founded from hopes of peace and order.

### The Age of Concordance
It was the scholar-sage [[Varcen the Arbiter]] who proposed an alternative. He observed that conflict, at its core, was not about force but about proving superiority—be it of wit, strength, or fortune. If superiority could be demonstrated without bloodshed, then perhaps war itself could be rendered obsolete. Thus, he devised [[Ludum Concordia|the Ludum Concordia]], a structured tradition where disputes would be settled not by blades or brawn but by games. This was an era of unity and order, when kingdoms, guilds, and city-states flourished through knowledge, diplomacy, and structured governance. Great cities like [[Calveris]] rose as centres of academia, magic, and governance.

### The Fracturing
For reasons still debated, the Age of Concordance came to an abrupt and violent end. Some say the ruling bodies grew corrupt, others whisper of an arcane catastrophe that shattered the balance of power. What is certain is that Yondar splintered—once-unified regions turned to feuding states, great libraries burned, and the [[The Arbiter Council|arbiters]] of [[Ludum Concordia|Ludum Concordia]] tightened their grip under the guise of maintaining peace.

### The Present Age – An Unstable Peace
Yondar stands at a tipping point. While the cities still thrive, tensions simmer beneath the surface of nations. The [[The Arbiter Council|arbiters]], once fair mediators, have grown increasingly authoritarian. New discoveries threaten the established order, and long-buried secrets are beginning to surface. Some whisper that the Fracturing was no accident, and that something old and patient waits beneath Yondar, ready to rise once more.

## [[Ludum Concordia]]
Ludum Concordia is not a single game but a principle—a contract between disputing parties to let fate, skill, or cleverness decide their grievance. The name comes from "Ludum" (game/play) with "Concordia" (harmony/agreement). Each nation in the region has developed its own unique form of Ludum Concordia, shaped by its culture and values.

Though the games differ, one rule remains constant: Once Ludum Concordia is invoked, both parties must abide by its outcome. These rules are adjudicated by the presence of the Arbiter Council. To reject one’s loss is to label oneself as an ‘Oathbreaker’ and risk exile or worse.

# The Popolis Despotate
A nation on the eastern end of [[Yondar]], the Popolis Despotate is a realm of intellect and authority, where knowledge is both currency and weapon. Ruled by the enigmatic [[The Argent Collegium|Argent Collegium]], this despotate holds an iron grip over the pursuit of wisdom, controlling not only the vast academic institutions of its capital, [[Calveris]], but also the flow of information, research, and magical advancements across its borders.

Nestled between rugged highlands to the north and fertile river valleys to the south, Popolis is a land of contrasts—where towering libraries and observatories stand in stark opposition to the squalor of knowledge-starved settlements on its outskirts. While its scholars delve into philosophy, alchemy, and arcane mechanics within the grand halls of learning, the common folk toil in regulated industries, their lives dictated by edicts drafted by unseen hands in Collegium chambers.

Despite its rigid hierarchy, Popolis is a beacon of progress. Its scholars push the boundaries of magic and science, producing ground-breaking advancements that ripple across Yondar. Yet, under the gleaming façade of intellectual prosperity lies a growing unrest—whispers of forbidden knowledge, suppressed discoveries, and a past the Collegium refuses to acknowledge. To those who dare to question the status quo, one thing is certain: knowledge is not free in the Popolis Despotate. It is a privilege—one that always comes at a cost.

# Calveris
Rising from the rolling hills of the [[Popolis Despotate]], Calveris is a city of labyrinthine streets and an endless hum of intellectual pursuit. The name of Calveris was derived from "calculus" (logic/math) and "veritas" (truth). It is a place where knowledge reigns supreme—a city of scholars, mages, inventors, and bureaucrats, all vying to push the boundaries of what is known. The air is thick with the scent of parchment, ink, and alchemical experiments, while the streets are alive with the murmur of debate, the scratching of quills, and the occasional arcane misfire.

At its peak stands the [[The Argent Collegium]], a sprawling institution of learning and governance, where the greatest minds of the region gather to study, lecture, and scheme. The city's districts are divided by specialised fields of study, with entire neighbourhoods dedicated to philosophy, arcane sciences, engineering, and history. Hidden alleys host forbidden discussions, underground presses publish unsanctioned theories, and in the backrooms of places unknown to the masses, those who see the cracks in the system gather to whisper about what comes next.

## Districts
### [[Darkgate]]
Tucked away in the southwestern corner of [[Calveris]], Darkgate is a district shrouded in secrecy and shadow. The scholars here pursue the dark arcane arts with a fervour bordering on obsession, delving into rituals and experiments that others deem too dangerous or unethical. As a result, much of Darkgate is bisected by towering city walls, forming a labyrinth of secured enclaves where research can be conducted beyond the gaze of the uninitiated. The air hums with residual magic, and flickering blue-green sigils ward doorways and alleys, warning outsiders to proceed at their own peril.

### [[Exilium]]
To the northeast of [[Calveris]] lies Exilium, a district of resentment and exile, where the city’s undesirables are left to fend for themselves. Magic is the lifeblood of [[Calveris]], but here, those without arcane talent or magical afflictions are pushed to the fringes, cast aside like discarded experiments. The streets are slick with toxic runoff, viscous puddles that bubble and swirl with unknown alchemical waste. Crumbling tenements lean against each other, their foundations eroded by the very magic that fuels the rest of the city. Crime festers in the alleys, and whispered deals are made in the smoky backrooms of grimy taverns. To walk these streets without purpose is to invite misfortune—or worse.

### [[Greybark Road]]
No street in [[Calveris]] pulses with life quite like Greybark Road. Renowned across the continent, this sprawling avenue is a mecca for all things arcane, where merchants and artificers peddle their wares in a cacophony of flashing enchantments and clanking automata. The air is thick with the scent of burning incense and arcane reagents, and the streets are perpetually crowded with seekers of magical power. Stalls overflow with floating grimoires, bottled storms, and spell-infused trinkets, while artificers proudly display clockwork thopters, steel golems, and arcane contraptions that defy explanation. To the unprepared, Greybark Road is a dizzying maze of flashing sigils, cryptic sales pitches, and half-heard deals whispered behind beaded curtains—but for those with coin and curiosity, there is no better place to uncover the city’s magical secrets.

### [[Luxor]]
At the heart of [[Calveris]] lies Luxor, the beating intellectual core of the city. Its pristine sandstone streets gleam under the sun, meticulously cleaned by unseen arcane forces, and its architecture is a testament to the scholarly pursuits that define [[Calveris]]. Towering spires and marble academies dominate the skyline, their halls filled with scribes, researchers, and apprentices debating the finer points of magical theory and philosophical paradoxes. Libraries stretch for city blocks, their shelves lined with forbidden tomes and cutting-edge research, while pristine lecture halls echo with heated scholarly disputes. Life here is dictated by the relentless pursuit of knowledge, innovation, and discovery, and those who walk these streets carry themselves with the quiet confidence of those who shape the future.

### [[Noonbridge]]
By all appearances, Noonbridge is the most unremarkable district in [[Calveris]]. Its well-paved suburban streets are lined with neatly kept homes, ornate gardens, and pristine lantern-lit walkways. Yet, behind the veneer of normalcy lies true power—this tiny, unassuming district is home to the most influential figures in the region. Within these walls, archmages, council leaders, and the city’s elite reside in cloistered luxury, far removed from the bustling chaos of the scholar’s city. The gates of Noonbridge are heavily guarded, patrolled by a relentless rotation of [[Calveris]]’ finest enforcers, who deny entry to all but the most essential visitors. Those who do manage to pass the watchful eyes of the gatekeepers might find themselves rubbing shoulders with figures who hold the fate of the continent in their hands.

### [[Oldgate]]
Perched just outside the south-eastern city walls of [[Calveris]], Oldgate is where tradition and discovery intertwine. Despite the uninspired name, this district is a cornerstone of botanical research, home to vast greenhouses, sprawling herbal gardens, and laboratories dedicated to the study of rare flora. The scent of fresh soil and exotic plants lingers in the air, and winding dirt roads lead out into fertile farmland, stretching over rolling hills as far as the eye can see. The scholars here seek to unravel the mysteries of nature—from alchemical herbs to magically infused crops—and their work supplies both the city and distant lands with potent elixirs and life-saving medicines. Though not as densely populated as other districts, Oldgate is quietly vital to [[Calveris]]’ survival.

### [[Omberlyn]]
Omberlyn exists in the periphery of memory, a district seldom spoken of and even less frequently visited. Officially, it is nothing more than an administrative oversight, a tangle of misplaced records and abandoned structures that the Collegium has deemed unworthy of attention. Yet, those who wander its mist-laden streets know better—Omberlyn is a secret buried in plain sight.

Nestled between Greybark Road and Darkgate in [[Calveris]], the district is a labyrinth of forgotten buildings, shuttered storefronts, and alleyways that seem to double back on themselves. Signs of habitation are subtle—a candle flickering behind cracked shutters, a worn path through the dust—but the residents here live as shadows, their existence undocumented, their identities unconfirmed.

The air is thick with the scent of damp parchment and old ink, as if the very walls remember the knowledge erased from history. Faded murals, their meanings long lost, stretch across crumbling facades, depicting constellations that no current star chart recognizes.

Those who seek forbidden knowledge, unregulated research, or those simply looking to disappear eventually find their way to Omberlyn. And once they do, they are faced with an unsettling realization—Omberlyn found them first.

## Key Locations

### [[The Fractured Theorem]]
Nestled in the heart of [Luxor](app://obsidian.md/Luxor), The Fractured Theorem is a well-known tavern for scholars, mages, and free thinkers. Unlike the quiet libraries and lecture halls of the city, this establishment is lively—filled with heated debates, drunken theorizing, and the occasional arcane mishap. The walls are adorned with half-finished equations, diagrams of magical phenomena, and notes scribbled by past patrons, some of which might even be useful (or wildly incorrect). The Fractured Theorem is the kind of place where professors drink alongside mercenaries, where students bet drinks over logic puzzles, and where new theories are born (and debunked) over a well-poured mug of ale.

The tavern specializes in unique drinks and experimental dishes, often inspired by alchemy and magical studies. The menu is a blend of local delicacies, exotic imports, and food inspired by academic disciplines—from mathematically perfect pie slices to magically aerated soufflés. While there are no rooms for lodging, The Fractured Theorem has plenty of dimly lit nooks and candlelit tables for scholars who need a place to talk, argue, or drink away the consequences of a failed experiment.

### [[The Obscured Quill]]
The forgotten pub, formerly known as the Obscured Quill, sits hidden in a small crevice of the former [Omberlyn](app://obsidian.md/Omberlyn) district. Once a sanctuary for those who lived between the lines, the pub now rots in silence. Dust chokes the air, broken glass litters the warped wooden floors, and overturned furniture suggests a departure both sudden and unwelcome. The scent of old ink and spilled spirits still lingers, a ghost of the secrets once exchanged here. A chalkboard menu, smeared and illegible, hangs askew behind the bar. A single chair stands upright in the centre of the room—as if someone was just here… or never left.

### [[The Sepulcrum Institute]]
Rising like a forgotten relic of an older age, The Sepulcrum Institute stands at the edge of Calveris’ sprawling districts, where the city’s ordered structures bleed into tangled, forgotten alleys. Its architecture is unsettling—a grim fusion of austere stone walls and dark iron latticework, betraying no warmth or welcome. The outer facade is hewn from deep grey basalt, its surfaces smoothed by time yet marred with ancient cracks, as though the building itself strains against something unseen.

Thin, elongated windows punctuate its walls, too high to peer into, their glass darkened with soot and age. Iron braziers, mounted at precise intervals, burn with pale blue flames that neither flicker nor wane—perpetual and cold. A singular, double-doored entrance, framed by intricate carvings of intertwined serpents, stands at the top of five shallow steps, its doors marked with symbols long since worn unreadable.

Despite its imposing silence, there is a hum in the air here—not sound, but something deeper. A pressure in the skull, a sense of being watched, though no sentries stand guard. Those who approach feel the weight of unseen eyes pressing against their skin, and those who linger too long find their thoughts drifting, disjointed, as if the very air conspires to unravel their intentions.

No sign identifies the building. No patients enter, and none are seen leaving.

### [[The Whispering Catacombs]]
The moment you cross its threshold, the air thickens—damp and cold, carrying the mineral scent of wet stone and something faintly metallic beneath it. The walls are carved from deep slate, slick with condensation, and warped by the slow breath of centuries. A low hum seems to vibrate just beyond hearing, rising and falling like distant chanting—or is it something deeper? Flickering green arcane lanterns float a few feet above the floor, tethered by no chains or spell sigils you can see. Their eerie glow casts warped shadows against the walls, making every alcove seem like a passage, and every turn like it’s watching you back. Dust motes hang frozen in the air, suspended in the faint, glowing haze.

### [[The Astral Observatory]]
Rising like a shard of ancient starlight from the northern ridge of Calveris, the Astral Observatory looms over the city like a silent sentinel of the skies. Its structure is elegant, geometric, and eerily pristine—three hexagonal tiers stacked atop one another, each level slightly smaller than the one below. From a distance, it resembles a crystalline tower or an abstract pillar of truth, etched against the firmament. The building is hewn from pale starstone—an iridescent mineral that shimmers faintly in the daylight and glows with a soft bluish-silver hue under moonlight. At each corner of the hexagonal walls, sleek bronze runes have been inlaid, pulsing ever so faintly with arcane charge. Thin, metallic rods protrude from the highest floor into the sky, forever reaching, measuring, and sometimes humming when the wind is right.

# Organisations
## [[The Arbiter Council]]
Throughout the land, Arbiters—neutral adjudicators trained in logic, strategy, and fairness—oversee the contests and ensure no one cheats. Their home base resides in the City of Burham, in the Bishopric of Chiltash.

### Structure and Hierarchy

#### The Grand Arbiter (Arbiter Prime)

**Role**: The supreme leader of the entire Ludum Concordia system. This Arbiter oversees all the cities, making the final decisions on disputes, game outcomes, and policies.  
**Powers**: Absolute authority over the Arbiter Council, able to manipulate the rules of Ludum Concordia across the region. They are the ultimate authority in deciding disputes and enforcing the system.  
**Corruption**: The Grand Arbiter may have started with noble intentions but has now turned into a power-hungry figure who controls the fate of the entire region through Ludum Concordia. Their corruption is the heart of the system.

#### High Arbiters (Arch-Arbiters)

**Role**: A council of elite Arbiters who hold sway over different regions or aspects of Ludum Concordia. These High Arbiters are responsible for overseeing large sections of the system, like specific cities or influential tournaments.  
**Powers**: They have significant influence within their assigned cities or domains and are responsible for carrying out the Grand Arbiter's commands. They may also act as judges in pivotal disputes or games.  
**Corruption**: Each High Arbiter uses their position to exploit their city's Ludum Concordia games for personal gain, whether it’s rigging outcomes, influencing trade, or favouring certain powerful factions.

#### Sentinels

**Role**: Trusted, senior agents who serve as the enforcement arm of the Council. They act as the enforcers of the Council’s decisions, ensuring that the rules of Ludum Concordia are followed.  
**Powers**: They oversee the enforcement of outcomes and ensure that those who violate the rules of the games (or refuse to comply with an Arbiter's ruling) are punished. They also ensure that any conflicts within the Council itself are kept hidden.  
**Corruption**: The Sentinels use fear, intimidation, and sometimes violence to maintain the system’s control. They may also be bribed by powerful factions to manipulate outcomes in favour of certain individuals.

#### Executors

**Role**: Junior members of the council who serve as administrators of the games and their outcomes. They are the ones who execute the decisions of the higher-ranking Arbiters and help maintain the day-to-day operations of the system.  
**Powers**: They may not have direct control over the region, but they can manipulate the specific details of each game (rules, participants, outcomes) to create a web of favouritism. They often act as intermediaries between the public and the higher Arbiters.  
**Corruption**: They are often swayed by bribes, promises of promotion, or threats from more powerful figures. Some Executors are notorious for subtly rigging games or influencing those who participate.

#### Initiates

**Role**: New members who are just starting out in the system. They serve in a learning and probationary capacity, working their way up through the ranks by proving their loyalty and efficiency in carrying out tasks.  
**Powers**: Limited, often working as assistants to Executors or Sentinels. They are learning the ways of the Council but have access to sensitive information and may be caught in the web of corruption.  
**Corruption**: The Initiates may be exploited or manipulated by more senior Arbiters, given the promise of higher status once they prove their worth. Some are used to carry out illicit tasks that ensure the system’s control remains intact.

## [[The Argent Collegium]]
The Argent Collegium is the unchallenged ruling body of the [Popolis Despotate](app://obsidian.md/Popolis%20Despotate), a council of scholars, arcane theorists, and bureaucratic masterminds who wield knowledge as both a shield and a weapon. More than just an academic institution, the Collegium is a machine of governance, ensuring that the pursuit of knowledge remains within the bounds of order, efficiency, and—above all—control.

The Argent Collegium operates on a singular belief: Knowledge is power, and power must be controlled. To the Collegium, unrestricted discovery leads to anarchy, and anarchy is a threat to civilization itself. Their rule is not one of open tyranny but of carefully engineered stability.

- Education is heavily regulated—while all citizens have access to basic learning, higher education is a privilege granted only to those who prove their loyalty and intellect.
- Magical research is restricted, with dangerous or reality-altering spells outlawed. Experimentation beyond sanctioned limits is met with severe punishment.
- The past is curated—historical records are maintained by the Lexoscribes, and some say certain truths have been erased from history entirely.

For all its structure and control, the Argent Collegium is not without cracks. Factions within the institution vie for influence, some advocating for greater freedom in research, while others push for even harsher restrictions. There are whispers of a schism among the Prime Magisters, a secret war fought not with steel, but with ideas, blackmail, and sabotage. Beyond its walls, dissent grows—unsanctioned researchers operate in the shadows, stolen manuscripts circulate in the black markets of Greybark Road, and rumours spread of a lost archive, where the Collegium’s greatest secrets are hidden.

### Structure & Hierarchy

The Collegium is a meritocratic autocracy, where power is earned through intellect, innovation, and political cunning. It is divided into several distinct orders, each overseeing different aspects of governance and research.

#### The Prime Magisters

The highest echelon of the Collegium, an elite council of twelve whose word is law. They control policy, regulate magical advancements, and dictate the direction of Popolis’ scholarly pursuits. Their identities are known, but their deliberations are conducted behind closed doors.

#### The Lexoscribes

Archivists, bureaucrats, and lawmakers responsible for drafting and enforcing the region’s laws. They oversee Exilium and maintain the Edicts of Knowledge, strict regulations on what research is permitted and what is deemed too dangerous.

#### The Luminaries

A sect of Collegium-sanctioned researchers, artificers, and mages who push the boundaries of magic and science—though only within the strict parameters set by the Prime Magisters. Breakthroughs are closely monitored, and unauthorized discoveries often lead to sudden disappearances.

#### The Concordants

The Collegium’s enforcers and inquisitors, who ensure that rogue scholars, heretical theories, and forbidden knowledge are swiftly dealt with. Often disguised as fellow academics, they root out dissent from within, ensuring absolute compliance.

# Key Characters
## [[Varcen the Arbiter]]
Once a respected and revered figure in the world of Ludum Concordia, Varcen Omirion was the original founder and Grand Arbiter of the system. His vision was simple yet profound: to create a world where differences could be settled through games rather than violence, where justice was based on skill, wit, and fairness. For many years, his dream flourished. Under his rule, Ludum Concordia became a beacon of order, bringing unity to fractured cities and factions. However, as time passed and the system expanded, the Council of Arbiters—once a noble group of impartial judges—became infiltrated by corruption. Wealth, power, and influence began to corrupt even the most principled Arbiters. The ideals Varcen had worked so hard to build were perverted, and his vision turned into a tool of control, oppression, and greed.

As the Arbiters’ greed grew, Varcen began to notice discrepancies in the games themselves. Initially, it was subtle—games manipulated in favour of wealthy merchants, outcomes skewed to benefit noble houses—but soon it escalated. The Arbiters were no longer just overseers; they were the puppet masters, controlling not only the games but the very fates of entire cities.

Varcen, a man who had dedicated his life to fairness, was horrified. He tried to expose the corruption to the public, but his words were dismissed as paranoid rants. The Arbiters, afraid of losing their power, turned on him. They branded him a traitor, accusing him of trying to undermine the very system he had created. In a matter of weeks, Varcen was cast aside, and a public trial was held. The Council used the occasion to solidify their power, portraying Varcen as a delusional figure who had lost his way.

Varcen’s exile was not merely physical; it was symbolic. He was erased from the history of Ludum Concordia, his name struck from every official record, his legacy tarnished. His once-illustrious title was reduced to a shadow, a cautionary tale to those who would question the will of the Council.

Now living in the shadows, Varcen remains a figure of legend—a ghost who walks the fringes of society. He is believed to be dead by most, but in truth, he has been carefully hiding in plain sight. He has lived in exile, moving between cities and settlements, gathering knowledge and allies, waiting for the right moment to strike back.

His time in hiding has not been without cost. The Arbiters’ Sentinels actively search for him, and he must constantly stay one step ahead of them. But in his exile, Varcen has grown wiser, more patient. He knows the Council’s weaknesses—their infighting, their greed, their fear of the truth coming to light—and he’s ready to use those against them.

He’s spent years compiling evidence of the Arbiters’ crimes, but without a trusted force to expose it, the corruption continues. Varcen now seeks the aid of outsiders, people who are not bound by the twisted laws of Ludum Concordia. He sees the players as his final hope—individuals capable of unravelling the web of corruption from within.

# The Story So Far
## Introducing the Heroes
The heroes were introduced with their respective opening sequences:
### Barnabus Dickus
Barnabus was in the Clockwork Bazaar, an area along [Greybark Road](app://obsidian.md/Greybark%20Road) filled with peculiar, magical, and mechanical oddities—artificers peddle their wares, alchemists sell questionable potions, and enchanted trinkets clink together in cluttered market stalls. He woke up locked in a storage crate after a night of drinking and gambling. The merchant who found him was furious and demanded payment. A bystander, amused by Barnabus’ predicament, jokingly suggested that “the only place to get a free drink after a bad bet is The Fractured Theorem.” With few other options, he heads there.

### Ezio Velore
Ezio awoke on an examination table with no memory of how he got there. The attending scholar was equally surprised to see him awake. Ezio persuaded the scholar to reveal information about the laboratory: A medical facility where scholars pushed the limits of magical and scientific discovery. Ethics are often debated, but rarely followed. A half-burned medical report found on the floor mentions a mysterious sponsor funding certain “experiments.” The sponsor’s last recorded location? [The Fractured Theorem](app://obsidian.md/The%20Fractured%20Theorem).

### Felicity Fartsalot
Felicity was hired to assist a stargazer obsessed with a constellation that shouldn’t exist. The night before, the stargazer entered the Astral Observatory and never came back out. Felicity's search for the astronomer led to rumours that he often drank at [The Fractured Theorem](app://obsidian.md/The%20Fractured%20Theorem) when his research frustrated him. When Felicity arrived, the bartender confirmed it—and says someone matching his description was last seen arguing with an Arbiter.

### Jombly Lane
A scholar paid Jombly to guard them during a ritual at [[The Whispering Catacombs]], but when he awoke, his employer was gone, and a strange message was scraped into the stone wall. A trail of chalk symbols—likely left by Jombly's employer—led him above ground and into the alleyway behind [[The Fractured Theorem]], where an anxious figure was waiting. But before he could speak, the figure vanished into the crowd.

### Morgan Cole
Morgan was at the Scholar's Bathhouse, a serene, marble-clad bathhouse where academics, researchers, and mages relaxed while discussing the latest theories and breakthroughs. While enjoying a well-earned rest, Morgan overhears two High Arbiters discussing a rigged Ludum Concordia game that resulted in someone’s disappearance. After leaving, a bathhouse attendant slipped Morgan a note, which warned him to "watch his back." The note is signed with the initials "[[Varcen the Arbiter|V.A.]]". A second note followed, instructing him to “seek answers where the broken gather.”

## The Fractured Theorem
Upon arriving at the Fractured Theorem, the heroes kerfuffle-d their ways through the tavern. A mysterious figure vanished, leaving behind a curious receipt listing: an unusual order, a name that cannot be deciphered, and a payment stamp from a pub known as the Obscured Quill in a district called Omberlyn that nobody seems to remember.

During their stay at the tavern, Barnabus continuously skulled down bottles and kegs of cruciferous liquor, with a thirst capable of drowning the whole region of Popolis. Morgan played the role of detective, albeit awkwardly due to his lack of intelligence. He managed to stumble through a horn based recreation of a classic piece of music, impress all who listened. Jombly pressed insistently upon the mysterious events leading up to and within the tavern, spurring on the investigation. Ezio remained cautious, proposing calculated suggestions to the party. Felicity - well… Felicity was there too.

At then end of the evening, the group received a map from the tavernkeeper and an intriguing set of clues from others:

_“Omberlyn holds the key.”  

“Start with what’s missing, not what’s there.”  

“Course, maps only tell you where things are—not where you ought to go. That part’s up to you.”

## Finding Varcen 
Previously in Calveris, the group continued with their investigation of the ominous receipt they found at [[The Fractured Theorem|the Fractured Theorem]]. Following the clues provided to them, they arrived in the forgotten district of [[Omberlyn]] and before the abandoned pub known as [[The Obscured Quill|the Obscured Quill]].

The group was ambushed by a band of Initiate Arbiters, one of whom oddly recognised [[Ezio Velore (James)|Ezio]]. [[Jombly Lane (Jewels)|Jombly]] desperately swung with his weapon, continuously embarrassing himself to the dismay of his colleagues. Likewise, [[Barnabus Dickus (Ashley)|Barny]]'s swing also consistently missed by hair-widths. Despite such arduous attempts at heroism, the group succeeded in defeating the Initiates, binding and questioning a survivor, who did not reveal much to the group.

After this dangerous encounter, a dusty bottle no longer holding its original contents revealed a note that led the heroes into the basement of the pub. At the bottom, they were met with puzzles of arcane nature, testing their wit and intuition in every chamber.

After overcoming the trials in the basement, the heroes come face to face with Varcen Omirion, who bestows upon them a crucial mission. To rid the continent of the now-corrupted grasp of the Arbiter Council. After a riveting speech, the heroes are teleported back to the streets of Luxor.

## The Fugitive
After the teleportation, the heroes are met with a distraught researcher being chased by two Concordants of the Argent Collegium.  They save the scholar by defeating the Concordants, and learn that the individual they had rescued was  [[Veylan Arros]], Jombly's missing employer. 

Veylan tells the group that he had escaped from a facility known as [[The Sepulcrum Institute]], where horrendously unethical research was being conducted on abducted individuals. The heroes decide to investigate this lead.

## The Sepulcrum Institute 
At the research facility, Ezio suggests to the group that he will stay behind to bring up the rear. The remaining heroes explore the institute, where they discover that the research being conducted involved investigations of consciousness by dissecting and looking at the anatomies of abducted species. These heroes manage to rescue abductees that were huddled in a corner of the building, guarded by several researchers and Husks - a zombiefied shell of what used to be a person. Amongst the abductees was [[Larethion Vensharde]], Felicity's missing mentor from the Astral Observatory. The heroes are almost defeated by a powerful researcher only to be rescued by the hidden Ezio, who reveals that he was dispatched by the Brotherhood on a secretive mission.

Upon returning to the Fractured Theorem with the rescued abductees, the heroes are directed by the tavernkeeper towards the back rooms, where a makeshift base has been set up under the instruction of Varcen. Larethion and Veylan recount their own respective experiences to the heroes. They reveal that the Arbiter Council ordered research regarding consciousness arcana in order to control the populace, and the scholars were captured for speaking against the research.  It is also revealed that the Arbiter Council was manipulating the Argent Collegium in backing this research. 

It is decided that together, this group will form a collective aimed at fighting against the corrupt Arbiter Council. The first order of business is for the heroes to collect two pieces of correspondence from the former "offices" of Larethion and Veylan, [[The Astral Observatory]] and [[The Whispering Catacombs]] respectively.

## Greybark Stalls
The group visit the [[Greybark Stalls]] to prepare themselves for the upcoming mission.

## Retrieving the Evidence
The group successfully defeat and collect the letters, each of the letters were written by a different Executor Arbiter: [[Wyn Ianberos]] and [[Ralul Paki]]. During their excursion to [[The Whispering Catacombs]] the heroes also managed to save some Initiate Arbiters that were surprised to learn of the groups' goal of destroying the Arbiter Council. It is revealed to the heroes that maybe some Arbiters are not evil, contrary to their original beliefs.

## The Scholar's Regret
On their way back to [[The Fractured Theorem]], the heroes bump into a scholar who was trying to incinerate a book, but the book would reappear nearby after alighting. The group deceive the scholar and take the mysterious book away.

## The Research Symposium
After returning to [[The Fractured Theorem]], Larethion and Veylan reveal that there is an upcoming research symposium, the main festival of Calveris, during which the Argent Collegium and Arbiter Council higher-ups will be attending to watch presentations of the most current research being conducted in the region. 

A plan to reveal their evidence of the Arbiter Council's research to the populace was thus hatched. The heroes were to present their evidence under the guise of 'current research' during the symposium.

After their presentation, Wyn and Ralul present themselves from amonst the audience, the heroes challenge them to a Ludum which the heroes won. However, despite having previously agreed on a wager, Jace, the Sentinel Arbiter that rules of the city of Calveris appears, instantaneously setting alight the first row of the audience members in order to trap the heroes onstage. 

The heroes fight Jace and win. Larethion and Veylan step in to temporarily replace the power vacuum that now exists in the Arbiter Council in Calveris.
