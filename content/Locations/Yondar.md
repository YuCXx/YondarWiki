Yondar is a land of contrasts and conflicts, innovation and ancient mysteries. Spanning vast territories, it is home to scholar cities, lawless frontiers, and forgotten ruins from civilizations long past. The land itself is known for its unpredictable geography, with landscapes shaped by both natural forces and arcane phenomena. Rivers shift course without warning, mountains seem to grow overnight, and hidden valleys appear only under the right celestial alignments.

## History
### The First Epoch – The Lost Age
Little is known about Yondar’s earliest inhabitants, save for the ruins they left behind—massive stone structures etched with unknown symbols, arcane conduits still humming with dormant energy, and buried archives of forgotten knowledge. Some scholars believe an ancient civilisation once wielded power over reality itself, while others argue that these ruins are the remnants of multiple lost cultures.

### The Rebirth
After the the First Epoch, the continent was plagued by endless feuds—between merchants disputing debts, noble houses vying for influence, and rival factions locked in cycles of revenge. Wars were waged over petty grievances, and blood stained the streets where words should have sufficed. It was during these tumultuous times that the four nations of the Popolis Despotate, the Kingdom of Zakrosia, the Nmoendan Theocracy, and Driecia were founded from hopes of peace and order.

### The Age of Concordance
It was the scholar-sage Varcen the Arbiter who proposed an alternative. He observed that conflict, at its core, was not about force but about proving superiority—be it of wit, strength, or fortune. If superiority could be demonstrated without bloodshed, then perhaps war itself could be rendered obsolete. Thus, he devised the Ludum Concordia, a structured tradition where disputes would be settled not by blades or brawn but by games. This was an era of unity and order, when kingdoms, guilds, and city-states flourished through knowledge, diplomacy, and structured governance. Great cities like Calveris rose as centres of academia, magic, and governance.

### The Fracturing
For reasons still debated, the Age of Concordance came to an abrupt and violent end. Some say the ruling bodies grew corrupt, others whisper of an arcane catastrophe that shattered the balance of power. What is certain is that Yondar splintered—once-unified regions turned to feuding states, great libraries burned, and the arbiters of Ludum Concordia tightened their grip under the guise of maintaining peace.

### The Present Age – An Unstable Peace
Yondar stands at a tipping point. While the cities still thrive, tensions simmer beneath the surface of nations. The Arbiter Council, once fair mediators, have grown increasingly authoritarian. New discoveries threaten the established order, and long-buried secrets are beginning to surface. Some whisper that the Fracturing was no accident, and that something old and patient waits beneath Yondar, ready to rise once more.

## Ludum Concordia
Ludum Concordia is not a single game but a principle—a contract between disputing parties to let fate, skill, or cleverness decide their grievance. The name comes from "Ludum" (game/play) with "Concordia" (harmony/agreement). Each nation in the region has developed its own unique form of Ludum Concordia, shaped by its culture and values.

Though the games differ, one rule remains constant: Once Ludum Concordia is invoked, both parties must abide by its outcome. These rules are adjudicated by the presence of the Arbiter Council. To reject one’s loss is to label oneself as an ‘Oathbreaker’ and risk exile or worse.
